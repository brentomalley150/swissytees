The Big Idea

We sell shirts with zero risk.

Print-on-demand means a partner only prints & ships a shirt after a customer pays. No buying shirts ahead of time, no inventory, no shipping ourselves. Declan focuses on designs and the brand — the printer does the rest. About half of every sale is profit.

🏪 Store Setup Plan

Step 1 — Print partner. Start with Printful (owns its own print shops → consistent quality, which fits our premium brand). Printify is a few dollars cheaper but quality varies — a good "add later" option. Recommended blank: the soft Bella+Canvas 3001 tee.

PrintfulPrintify
Base cost (quality tee)~$12–13~$8–11
Quality controlOwns print shopsOutside printers (varies)
Best forQuality & easeWider margins later

Step 2 — Where customers check out. Launch on Etsy first (no monthly fee, brings its own dog-and-gift shoppers, fast first sale) while setting up Shopify on swissytees.com in parallel as the real flagship. Many small brands run both — Etsy for discovery, your own site for brand & repeat buyers.

Step 3 — Connect the dots. Create Printful → upload designs onto the tee → connect to Etsy/Shopify → set prices → publish 3–5 starter products → order 1 sample to check quality and photograph (with Heinz!) → flip swissytees.com to the live store → email the launch list with a discount code.

🎨 First Drop — 5 Designs

Start with 3–5 strong designs, not 30. A tight first drop beats a cluttered shop.

Widest audience · most buyers search "Bernese"

1. The Hero Bernese Tee

Clean, friendly illustrated Bernese (fluffy tricolor), art-forward, minimal text. The flagship.

Funny & shareable on social

2. "Owned by a Swissy"

Bold text design with a small paw print or dog silhouette. Easy gift, travels on TikTok/Instagram.

Brand story · the winner

3. Heinz / Evergreen Alpine Badge

The logo as a wearable — dog on a mountain overlook, "Evergreen, Colorado" in the ring. A nod to Heinz.

Broadens to general buyers

4. "Big Dogs. Bigger Hearts."

The tagline in a warm, clean layout with a small Swiss-cross or paw accent. Wearable even by non-owners.

Give-back & loyalty

5. The Rescue Tee

"$X from every shirt supports Swiss Mountain Dog rescue." Pick one rescue and name it.

Keep it on-brand: badge palette (navy, rust/tan, white, Swiss red), clean breed-accurate art, dog is the hero. Offer each on cream, white, and heather navy.

💰 Pricing & Money Plan

Sell at $28 — right in the pocket for a premium dog tee (rivals sell $26–34), and positioned as quality, not bargain-bin. Customers pay shipping, so it doesn't eat profit.

Startup cost: about $20–30 total — one sample shirt (~$15) plus a few Etsy listings (~$0.20 each). Shirts themselves cost $0 because of print-on-demand.

First goal: sell 10 shirts ≈ $140 profit, plus real photos and first reviews. Then reinvest a little into the next sample and a small social push. Small, real, repeatable.
