# Swissytees — Brand Foundation

*Apparel for people who love Swiss Mountain Dogs.*
Version 1.0 · June 2026

---

## 1. The one-line pitch

**Swissytees makes wearable love letters to the four Swiss Mountain Dog breeds — soft, well-made tees and merch that let owners and admirers show their devotion without looking like a gift-shop bargain bin.**

---

## 2. Why this brand can win

The dog-apparel market is large and growing (the global dog clothing market is projected to pass **$7B by 2028**), and the fastest-rising slice is *dog-brand clothing for the humans* — owner tees, hoodies, and totes — with related search interest up roughly **29% year over year.** But the Swiss Mountain Dog corner of that market is crowded with sameness: generic clip-art Bernese tees dumped onto Zazzle, Amazon, and CafePress with no point of view, no quality story, and no community.

That's the opening. Swissytees wins not by being the only Swiss-dog shirt, but by being the *good* one — a real brand with a distinct look, a warm voice, premium feel, and genuine love for the breeds, rather than a print-on-demand catalog. We treat these dogs as the gentle giants and family members they are, not as a search keyword.

---

## 3. What "Swissy" means (and why it's our edge)

"Swissy" is dog-world slang for the **Greater Swiss Mountain Dog**, but the four working dogs of the Swiss Alps are a family — the *Sennenhund* breeds:

- **Bernese Mountain Dog** (Berner Sennenhund) — the famous fluffy tricolor; by far the most popular.
- **Greater Swiss Mountain Dog** (Grosser Schweizer Sennenhund) — the original "Swissy," biggest of the four.
- **Appenzeller Sennenhund** — agile, energetic herder.
- **Entlebucher Mountain Dog** (Entlebucher Sennenhund) — smallest and quickest.

**Strategic angle:** Most competitors only serve Bernese owners. Swissytees can own the *whole family* of Swiss Mountain Dogs — starting with the popular Bernese for volume, but giving the rarer three breeds the loving, breed-accurate designs almost nobody else makes. That's an underserved, fiercely loyal audience and a built-in expansion path.

## 4. Mission, vision, values

**Mission** — To make apparel that captures the heart of Swiss Mountain Dogs so well that wearing it feels like a hug from your dog.

**Vision** — To become *the* name dog people think of for Swiss Mountain Dog apparel and gifts — the brand that proves a niche dog tee can be beautifully designed, ethically made, and worth keeping.

**Values**
- **Craft over clip-art.** Original artwork and quality blanks, never lazy stock graphics.
- **Breed-true.** Markings, builds, and personalities drawn right — owners notice.
- **Warm, not corny.** Affectionate humor, never cheesy or mean.
- **Give back.** A meaningful share of profits supports Swiss Mountain Dog rescue (a proven, beloved move in this community).

---

## 5. Target customer

**Primary — The Devoted Owner.** Owns one or more Swiss Mountain Dogs (most often a Bernese). The dog is family. They already follow breed accounts, buy themed mugs and stickers, and will happily pay a premium for something that actually looks like *their* dog. Skews 25–55, disproportionately women, active on Instagram/TikTok.

**Secondary — The Gift Buyer.** Spouse, kid, parent, or friend buying for a Swissy owner. Needs an obvious, safe, delightful gift — strong driver in Q4 and around birthdays.

**Tertiary — The Admirer / Aspirer.** Loves the breed, maybe planning to get one, drawn to the cottagecore/alpine aesthetic. A broader, more casual buyer that lets us grow beyond current owners.

Because the brief is **general consumers**, the look should read as genuinely good apparel first and dog merch second — something a buyer would wear even if a stranger didn't know the breed.

## 7. Brand personality & voice

If Swissytees were a person: the friend who loves their dog more than most people, has great taste, and makes you laugh without trying too hard. Warm, a little witty, design-literate, and unmistakably sincere about the dogs.

**Voice principles**
- **Affectionate, not saccharine.** "Big dog. Bigger heart." — not "fur baby" overload.
- **Witty, not gimmicky.** A light pun is welcome; a groan-worthy one is not.
- **Plainspoken and confident.** Short sentences. Real words. No marketing fog.
- **Insider-warm.** Speak like someone who actually owns the breed (drool, shedding, leaning, the 100-lb lapdog) — details signal "one of us."

**Sounds like:** "Professionally trained to do nothing." · "Yes, he sheds. No, I don't care." · "Three colors. One whole heart."
**Never sounds like:** clinical breed-encyclopedia copy, or hard-sell discount-blast hype.

---

## 8. Name rationale

**Swissytees** works on three levels: it names the *breed* (Swissy), the *product* (tees), and reads as friendly and approachable. It's short, brandable, and memorable. Note one nuance to lean into rather than hide: "Swissy" technically means the Greater Swiss Mountain Dog, while most buyers arrive via Bernese — our messaging should embrace *all four* Swiss breeds so the name feels inclusive rather than narrow. Treat "Swissy" as an affectionate umbrella term for the whole Sennenhund family.

*(Worth a quick check before launch: secure swissytees.com / .co, the Instagram + TikTok handles, and a basic trademark search.)*

## 10. Visual identity direction

**Mood:** Alpine warmth meets clean modern apparel brand. Think cozy Swiss cottage, fresh snow, fireside — not neon novelty tee.

**Color palette (starting point)**

| Role | Color | Hex | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Swiss Charcoal | `#2B2B2B` | The tricolor black; grounding, premium |
| Primary | Rust / Tan | `#A0522D` | The breed's signature rust markings |
| Accent | Alpine Red | `#D32F2F` | Nods to the Swiss flag; energy + CTA |
| Neutral | Cream / Snow | `#F4EFE6` | Soft, cozy background |
| Neutral | Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | The chest blaze; clean space |

The black / rust / white trio doubles as the **Bernese tricolor** *and* a sophisticated apparel palette — that's the visual hook. Alpine Red ties back to Switzerland for accents and packaging.

**Logo direction**
- A clean, friendly **wordmark** ("swissytees") as the primary mark — lowercase, rounded-but-confident sans serif, approachable.
- A **mascot/emblem mark** for hangtags, hats, and the website favicon: a simple, modern Swiss Mountain Dog silhouette or head — preferably stylized enough to imply any of the four breeds, with a small Swiss-cross or alpine cue.
- Keep designs **art-forward**: the dog artwork is the hero on the garment; the logo stays small and tasteful (sleeve, hem, or back-neck).

**Garment standard:** premium soft blanks (e.g., combed ring-spun cotton / quality triblends), thoughtful color options, true-to-breed art. The unboxing and feel should say "keep this," not "stocking stuffer."

## 11. Initial product & range ideas

- **Hero breed tees** — one strong, original design per breed (lead with Bernese), expandable into a signature illustrated series.
- **"Owned by a Swissy" line** — the witty owner-humor capsule for social shareability.
- **Alpine heritage line** — the cottagecore/Swiss-flag aesthetic for the admirer/aspirer audience.
- **Beyond tees** — hoodies, hats, totes, stickers, and mugs (proven sellers and easy entry-price gifts).
- **Rescue collection** — a design where a defined share funds Swiss Mountain Dog rescue, made explicit on the product page.
